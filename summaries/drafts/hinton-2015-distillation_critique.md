1. **Weighted objective description is incorrect.**  
   - The paper states that the best results are obtained by using a *considerably lower weight on the hard target objective* (the second objective function). The summary’s formula `L = α * L_soft + (1-α) * L_hard` with α “typically small, e.g., 0.5” contradicts this: a small α would give less weight to soft targets, not hard targets. In the speech experiment, the paper uses a relative weight of 0.5 on the hard targets, but the general recommendation is a lower weight on hard targets. The summary should reflect that the weight on hard targets is smaller, and the soft target loss is scaled by `T^2` (see next point). The example α=0.5 is not representative of a “small” weight on soft targets.

2. **Gradient scaling description is misleading.**  
   - The paper says: “Since the magnitudes of the gradients produced by the soft targets scale as `1/T^2` it is important to multiply them by `T^2` when using both hard and soft targets.” The summary states “The gradients from the soft targets are scaled by `1/T^2` to maintain consistent relative contributions,” which is the opposite of the actual practice. The correct interpretation is that the soft target loss is multiplied by `T^2` to compensate for the `1/T^2` scaling of its gradients, thereby keeping the relative contributions of hard and soft targets roughly constant when `T` changes.

3. **Specialist model training parallelization is misrepresented.**  
   - The summary claims “The specialist model training is **not parallelizable** in the same way as standard ensemble training (requires a generalist first).” The paper explicitly states: “It is much easier to parallelize the training of multiple specialists. … Once these subsets have been defined the specialists can be trained entirely independently.” While a generalist must be trained first, the specialists themselves can be trained in parallel. The summary should note that specialist training is parallelizable after the generalist is obtained, not that it is non-parallelizable.

These corrections are necessary to accurately reflect the paper’s methodology. The mathematical formulas (softmax with temperature, gradient of soft target loss, high‑temperature approximation, specialist inference) are otherwise correctly transcribed.
